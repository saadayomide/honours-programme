Salamanca 2030

Salamanca will become Madrid’s first neighborhood designed for people

Cleaner streets, safer spaces for families, and stronger local businesses.

A project of the Ayuntamiento de Madrid for the future of the Salamanca district.

A message from the Mayor of Madrid

Salamanca is one of the most vibrant and important districts in Madrid. More than 147,000 residents live in just 5.38 km², making it one of the most densely populated districts in the city.

Our goal is simple: a Salamanca where children can play safely, where elderly residents can move easily, where businesses thrive, and where the air we breathe is cleaner. Not by banning cars, but by designing smarter streets that work for everyone.

The Three Promises

Better Life for Residents

“Your neighborhood becomes calmer, greener, and safer.”

“More people on the streets means more customers for our local businesses.”

Mobility for Everyone

  • not anti-car — smarter transport

“Residents keep access, but traffic chaos disappears.”

Why change is necessary

In Madrid today, the city has over 3.3 million inhabitants. Dense districts like Salamanca experience constant traffic pressure and public space is dominated by vehicles instead of people.

International studies show that redesigning urban streets can significantly improve quality of life. Research on European pedestrianized districts shows that walkable streets increase retail activity and attract more customers to local businesses. Cities like Barcelona have demonstrated that reducing car dominance in residential areas can improve air quality, physical activity, public health and social interaction.

Salamanca has the opportunity to become Madrid’s first circular mobility neighborhood, combining better mobility, stronger commerce, greener streets and healthier living conditions.

Our Vision

Salamanca will become Madrid’s first Circular Mobility District – a district where residents move easily, businesses attract more customers, public spaces belong to people and mobility is cleaner and smarter, without eliminating access for residents or essential services.

What Salamanca Could Look Like

  • tree-lined streets where families walk safely
  • local markets and terraces full of life
  • elderly residents moving easily with neighborhood minibuses
  • quieter evenings and cleaner air

Imagine walking through Salamanca on a Sunday morning. Children playing in safe streets, local markets full of life, cafés with terraces full of people, and cleaner air for everyone. That is the Salamanca we want to build together.

Salamanca Circular Mobility Plan – Final Policy Measures

The Salamanca Circular Mobility Plan is structured around ten key measures that rebalance streets, improve mobility and strengthen public life.

1. Avenue Rebalancing Program

Streets concerned: Calle Serrano, Calle Velázquez, Calle Conde de Peñalver.

One vehicle lane on each of these avenues will be transformed into a multi-use urban corridor with protected cycling lanes, trees and green infrastructure, shaded seating, small exercise spaces for elderly residents and small play spaces for children. The objective is to rebalance the street to serve both mobility and public life while maintaining vehicle circulation.

2. Príncipe de Vergara Central Corridor & Innovation Roundabout

Príncipe de Vergara will be reorganized to create a central public corridor with vegetation, exercise areas, pedestrian resting areas and cycling infrastructure, while keeping traffic lanes on both sides. One of the central roundabouts will become a mini urban park dedicated to innovation and entrepreneurship, hosting startup exhibitions, urban innovation showcases, temporary installations and public events.

3. Residential Street Rebalancing

On internal neighborhood streets where parking currently exists on both sides, one parking row will be removed and replaced with a protected cycling lane, vegetation and trees, and wider pedestrian areas. One parking row remains available to residents, improving street quality while maintaining parking.

4. Salamanca Cycling Network

A continuous protected cycling network will be created using the redesigned avenues and residential streets. The network will connect Salamanca with Retiro, Chamberí, central Madrid and key metro stations, providing a safe and efficient alternative for short urban trips.

5. Electric Neighborhood Mini-Bus System

A small electric mini-bus network will circulate inside the district, connecting residential streets, metro stations, commercial areas and external parking hubs. The system prioritizes elderly residents, families and people with reduced mobility to ensure convenient mobility without dependence on private cars.

6. External Parking Hub Strategy

Partnerships will be developed with parking operators near Madrid’s M-30 ring road. Visitors arriving by car will park outside the district and access Salamanca via metro, buses and the neighborhood mini-bus system. The program aims to reduce internal street parking demand by approximately 50%, cutting congestion while maintaining accessibility.

7. Restricted Vehicle Access Policy

Access inside Salamanca will be limited to residents, taxis and ride-sharing services, delivery vehicles, emergency services and public transport. All other vehicles will be redirected to external parking hubs. The objective is to reduce unnecessary traffic circulation while maintaining essential access.

8. Sunday Open Streets Program

Selected main streets will be closed to private vehicles every Sunday and opened for local street markets, stands for neighborhood businesses, and cultural and community events. This supports local commerce, encourages community life and activates public space.

9. Smart Traffic Management

AI-based traffic light systems will be installed on the perimeter roads surrounding Salamanca to optimize traffic flow, reduce congestion on boundary streets and improve bus reliability.

10. Urban Innovation and Design Program

Urban design competitions will be launched to rethink the new public spaces. Architects, universities, startups and urban designers will be invited to participate, encouraging innovation and citizen participation in shaping Salamanca’s future.

Implementation Timeline

  1. Phase 1 – Pilot projects and street redesign trials.
  2. Phase 2 – Expansion of mobility infrastructure.
  3. Phase 3 – Full district implementation.
  4. Phase 4 – Evaluation and potential expansion to other districts.

Funding

The project will combine Madrid municipal investment, European funding programs such as the European Regional Development Fund (ERDF), and partnerships with private mobility and parking operators.
