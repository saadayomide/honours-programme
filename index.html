A message from the Mayor

Salamanca is one of the most vibrant districts in Madrid. More than 147,000 residents live in just 5.38 km², making it one of the most densely populated areas in the city.

Our goal is simple: a Salamanca where children can play safely, elderly residents move easily, businesses thrive and the air we breathe is cleaner. Not by banning cars, but by designing smarter streets that work for everyone.

Why Change Is Necessary

Madrid has over 3.3 million inhabitants. Dense districts like Salamanca experience constant traffic pressure and public space is dominated by vehicles instead of people.

International studies show that redesigning urban streets significantly improves quality of life. Research on European pedestrianized districts demonstrates that walkable streets increase retail activity and attract more customers. Cities like Barcelona have shown that reducing car dominance improves air quality, physical activity, public health and social interaction.

Our Vision

Salamanca will become Madrid's first Circular Mobility District — where residents move easily, businesses attract more customers, public spaces belong to people and mobility is cleaner and smarter, without eliminating access for residents or essential services.

"Imagine walking through Salamanca on a Sunday morning. Children playing in safe streets, local markets full of life, cafés with terraces full of people, and cleaner air for everyone. That is the Salamanca we want to build together."
The Program

Salamanca Circular Mobility Plan

Ten coordinated measures to rebalance streets, improve mobility and strengthen public life.

01

Avenue Rebalancing Program

Calle Serrano, Calle Velázquez, Calle Conde de Peñalver. One vehicle lane transformed into a multi-use urban corridor with protected cycling lanes, trees, shaded seating, exercise spaces for elderly residents and play spaces for children.

02

Príncipe de Vergara Central Corridor

A central public corridor with vegetation, exercise and resting areas, and cycling infrastructure. One roundabout becomes a mini urban park for innovation — startup exhibitions, showcases and public events.

03

Residential Street Rebalancing

On internal streets with parking on both sides, one parking row is replaced with a protected cycling lane, vegetation and wider pedestrian areas. One row remains for residents.

04

Salamanca Cycling Network

A continuous protected cycling network connecting Salamanca with Retiro, Chamberí, central Madrid and key metro stations — a safe alternative for short urban trips.

05

Electric Mini-Bus System

Small electric mini-buses circulating inside the district, connecting residential streets, metro stations, commercial areas and parking hubs — prioritizing elderly residents, families and people with reduced mobility.

06

External Parking Hub Strategy

Partnerships with parking operators near the M-30. Visitors park outside the district and access Salamanca via metro, buses and mini-buses. Target: reduce internal parking demand by 50%.

07

Restricted Vehicle Access

Access limited to residents, taxis, deliveries, emergency services and public transport. All other vehicles redirected to external parking hubs.

08

Sunday Open Streets

Selected main streets closed to private vehicles every Sunday for local markets, neighborhood business stands and cultural events.

09

Smart Traffic Management

AI-based traffic light systems on perimeter roads to optimize flow, reduce congestion and improve bus reliability.

10

Urban Innovation & Design

Design competitions for new public spaces — inviting architects, universities, startups and urban designers to shape Salamanca's future.
